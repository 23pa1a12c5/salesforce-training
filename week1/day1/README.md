Salesforce Summer Program – Day 1

1. What is CRM
CRM stands for Customer Relationship Management. This technology allows you to manage relationships with your customers and prospects and track data related to all of your interactions. It also helps teams collaborate, both internally and externally, gather insights from social media, track important metrics, and communicate via email, phone, social, and other channels.

2. Why Companies Use Salesforce

Companies use Salesforce because it:

 - Stores customer information centrally
 - Improves communication with customers
 - Helps track sales and opportunities
 - Automates business processes
 - Provides reports and analytics
 - Supports customer service management

Accounts and Contacts
Remember that when a lead is converted, an account and contact are also created in Salesforce. An account is a company you’re doing business with, and a contact is someone who works at that account. Whenever your sales reps drill into an account or contact, they need to find the details they’re looking for quickly, but they probably don’t need to change those details all that often. So we’ve optimized the layout for these pages for quick reference, allowing your sales reps to find information and gather insight at-a-glance.

Opportunity
An Opportunity represents a potential sales deal or business transaction.

  Example: A company planning to purchase software services
